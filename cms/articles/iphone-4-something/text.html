Apple's latest iPhone, the iPhone 5 has broken the 5 million mark in the opening weekend, putting it in a clear lead against its closest competitor, the Samsung Galaxy SIII, and beating its previous iteration the iPhone 4S, which sold just over 4 million. Much to the disdain of some critics and analysts, that claim the latest iPhone is "boring" and "disappointing". It's clear by the numbers that Apple's iterative annual approach to phone upgrades is effective, and its reflected in Samsung's latest strategy of releasing a yearly flagship Galaxy S.

How the iPhone's sales progress in the long run remains to be seen, but the holiday season is coming up and we predict it won't be slowing down for a while.
